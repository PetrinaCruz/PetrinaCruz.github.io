Understanding SEO and How It Can Drive Traffic To Your Website


Search Engine Optimization (SEO) - The Basics

Understanding all of the components of SEO is an extensive topic, so the purpose of this article is to recomment a few tips and references to help you develop an SEO plan to optimize the content of your webpage. Keep in mind that the main idea is think about your target audience and determine the best ways to get them to your website.

  • Start by learn the basic concepts and terminology of SEO. Moz is one of the top companies in the field of SEO and it offers an online "SEO 101: Beginner's Guide to SEO" that provides a fairly solid overview. Starting with search engine basics, explaining organic search results, and basic Google (and Bing) Webmaster Guidelines. There is also a helpful glossary.
  • Once you have an better understanding of SEO, make an On-page SEO checklist. On-page SEO referes to optimizing the content on your webpage. This example list is based on the On-page SEO checklist from the Ahrefs blog.
    1. Start with descriptive URL, but keep it short. A clear descriptive URL will help serchers understand what your webpage is about in the Search Engine Results Pages (SERPs).
    2. Choose a compelling title tag. This will also help searchers understand what the content of your webpage is about. It's a good practice to use your target keyword in your title tag.
    3. Create a meta description that expands on the title and content that searchers will be looking for.
    4. Link to relevant internal and/or external resources when it makes sense to do so.
    5. Optimize the images on your webpages. Use descriptive filenames and alt text names.
  • Continue to keep up with SEO updates. As technologies advance, new best practices for SEO will be developed. Updated content like Julia Nesterets' TechRadar article "8 SEO techniques to increase website traffic in 2021", covers some of the updates to SEO practices.

I hope that this article has inspired you to use these resources and start researching additional information to develop an SEO plan for your website.

Good luck!
